What stands out most in Marseille’s 2025 property market is the gap between what sellers ask and what buyers actually pay. Listings are averaging around **€2,400/m²**, but recent sales are closing much higher at **€3,223/m²** — a **25.5% premium**. That’s a strong sign that the market is firmer than portal prices suggest.

At the same time, Marseille still looks affordable on paper compared with many major French cities. Broad listing stock averages just **€1,675/m²**, which can make the city seem like a bargain. But the transaction data tells a different story: the homes that are actually selling are not sitting at the low end of the market.

For buyers, the takeaway is simple: don’t anchor your budget to asking prices alone. A **50 m²** apartment priced at €2,400/m² would cost **€120,000**, but at €3,223/m² it jumps to **€161,150** — a difference of more than **€41,000**. In other words, Marseille may still be one of France’s more accessible big-city markets, but the best properties are clearly commanding a premium.
