The biggest surprise in Italy’s apartment market snapshot is how extreme the yield spread is: Messina tops the chart at 8.63%, while Cesena lands all the way down at 2.83%. That’s a huge gap for a single national market, and it shows how differently asking prices and rents can line up from city to city.

On the high-yield side, the pattern is pretty clear: relatively affordable purchase prices paired with solid rents. Messina combines a median asking sale price of €95,214 with rent at €685/month, while Taranto posts 7.55% on just €86,425 in median asking price and €544/month in rent. Genova also stands out at 7.72%, proving this isn’t just a small-city story.

The interesting part is the mix of markets. The outliers include both larger urban areas like Genova and smaller centers such as Brindisi, Terni, and Catanzaro, all above 7%. That makes the list useful for investors scanning nationally: unusually strong yields can show up in mainstream cities, not just niche markets.
