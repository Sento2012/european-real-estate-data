Spain’s biggest housing surprise isn’t that prices rose — it’s that the upswing *didn’t* fade in late 2025. Instead, Spain’s housing market kept accelerating, with INE data showing a clear move from mid-single-digit annual gains in early 2024 to sustained double-digit growth by the end of 2024 and well into 2025.

The numbers tell the story. Early 2024 annual house price growth was mostly in the **4.70% to 7.90%** range, but by **Q4 2024** it had jumped to **8.60% to 13.40%**. Momentum then carried into 2025, when annual growth stayed between **10.00% and 15.30%** across the year. Quarterly changes also remained positive, reaching as high as **5.10%** in **Q1 2025**.

That’s a classic sign of a market still climbing, not one topping out. Even in **Q4 2025**, prices were still advancing quarter over quarter, with gains of **0.30% to 3.00%**, suggesting the rally had real staying power rather than just a short-lived spike.

For buyers, investors, and policymakers, the takeaway is simple: Spain’s housing market entered 2025 with strong momentum and never really gave it back.
