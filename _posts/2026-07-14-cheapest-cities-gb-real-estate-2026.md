The most surprising takeaway? Britain’s cheapest apartment markets aren’t just cheap — some are also unusually strong income plays. In the 27 Jun 2026 snapshot, several of the lowest-priced cities paired sub-€100,000 median asking prices with double-digit gross yields, making them stand out from the rest of the market.

Middlesbrough was the clear entry-price leader at €79,174 across 112 listings. Bradford and Sunderland followed at €85,911, with 207 and 193 listings respectively, while Stoke on Trent came in at €92,650. Aberdeen also stayed below the €100,000 mark at €99,388, showing that affordability is spread across northern England and Scotland rather than concentrated in just one region.

There’s also a noticeable step up after the top five: Blackpool sits at €102,758, Preston at €116,233, and Wolverhampton reaches €133,080. For buyers on a strict budget, that gap matters — it can mean a very different deposit requirement even before mortgage costs are considered.

In short, the cheapest apartment markets are clustered in the North, North East, Scotland and the Midlands, but the best value isn’t always the lowest sticker price. Some cities offer a better balance of affordability and rental performance than others.
