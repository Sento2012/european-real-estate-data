The most striking takeaway is how quickly French apartment yields thin out as size increases: 1-room units average a solid 6.38% gross yield, but that drops to just 3.68% for 5+ room apartments. In other words, the bigger the home, the weaker the income return — even though the purchase price keeps climbing.

The price ladder is just as clear. Median sale prices rise from €123,046 for 1-room apartments to €180,175 for 2-room units, then jump to €250,487 for 3-room homes. By the time you reach 5+ room apartments, the median price is €487,792, showing how quickly larger family-sized stock pushes buyers into a much higher capital commitment.

For investors, this creates a straightforward trade-off: smaller apartments offer stronger yield, while larger ones demand more cash but deliver less rental income relative to price. That’s a useful reminder that “more space” doesn’t necessarily mean “better return” in the French apartment market.
