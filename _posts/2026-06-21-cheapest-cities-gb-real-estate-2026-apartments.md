The most surprising thing? Even the cheapest UK apartment markets are still clustered tightly together: the entire top 10 tops out at just €126,341, and Middlesbrough leads the pack at only €79,174. For buyers hunting for an entry point, that’s a stark reminder that affordability still exists — but mostly in a few specific regional pockets.

The pattern is clear: low prices are concentrated in northern England, the North East, and parts of the Midlands. Sunderland and Bradford both came in at €85,911, while Stoke on Trent followed at €89,281. Aberdeen also made the sub-€100,000 club at €99,388, showing that cheap apartment stock isn’t limited to one region.

What stands out is how quickly prices rise after that. Blackpool sits at €102,758, Preston at €116,233, and the remaining cities in the top 10 all cluster around €126,341. For budget-conscious buyers, the message is simple: if you want the lowest asking prices, you need to focus on a narrow set of markets where entry costs remain materially below the UK average.
