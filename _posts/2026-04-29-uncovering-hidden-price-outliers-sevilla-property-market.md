Sevilla’s most surprising housing signal isn’t what’s cheap — it’s that there are **no significantly cheap outliers at all**. In a city this size, you’d expect at least a few bargain listings below the norm, but JobStatsen’s data shows a market floor that looks remarkably firm.

The numbers make the pattern clear: the **median asking price is €1,928 per m²**, there are **0 cheap outliers**, and there are **15 expensive outliers** sitting above the market trend. That imbalance suggests sellers are holding prices steady on the low end while premium homes keep stretching the top end.

For buyers, especially those watching affordability, that’s a tough mix. It means there are fewer hidden discounts to uncover, and the market isn’t offering many mispriced opportunities to offset rising pressure. In other words, Sevilla looks less like a place with pockets of value and more like a market where the baseline has tightened.
