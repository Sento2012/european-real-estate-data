Berlin’s most surprising spring 2026 signal wasn’t a price drop — it was a yield squeeze. Even with the median asking sale price holding steady at **€398,863** across every weekly snapshot, gross apartment yield still fell from **2.54%** to **2.39%** between 22 April and 6 June.

What changed was rent. The median asking rent slipped from **€844/month** at the start of the series to **€795/month** by late May, with the decline unfolding in steps: first flat, then down to **€834**, then **€815**, and finally **€795**. That pattern suggests the market’s income side softened faster than its asset prices, which is exactly what compressed returns.

The yield story itself was pretty clean: **2.54%** on 22 April, still **2.54%** on 2 May, then down to **2.51%**, **2.45%**, and finally **2.39%** by 23 May, where it stayed through 6 June. In other words, Berlin moved from a stable opening to a lower plateau in just over a month.
