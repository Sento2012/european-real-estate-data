The most surprising finding in the French housing data is that bigger apartments can be dramatically cheaper per square meter than smaller ones. That flips the usual market logic on its head, and it’s not a one-off: JobStatsen identified **59 size-based pricing anomalies** across France.

The biggest gaps show up in major cities. In **Lyon 1st arrondissement**, median prices drop from **€4,456/m²** for smaller apartments to **€634/m²** for larger ones, an **85.8% discount**. In **Paris 2nd**, the shift is from **€9,058/m²** to **€1,809/m²**. **Rouen** also stands out, with larger units priced at just **€271/m²** versus **€1,122/m²** for smaller homes.

What this suggests is a real opportunity for buyers who can stretch beyond the studio and compact-apartment segment. In several French urban markets, the premium for size appears to vanish—and in some cases, reverse entirely—making larger homes surprisingly attractive on a value-per-square-meter basis.
