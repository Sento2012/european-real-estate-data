The biggest surprise in this June 2026 snapshot is how close several French cities get to a 10% gross yield on studios and 1-room apartments. That’s not a niche outlier either: Valence, Perpignan, and Saint-Étienne all sit at the top of the ranking, showing that small-unit investing can still look very attractive when purchase prices stay low.

Valence leads with a 9.85% gross yield, helped by a median asking price of €54,198 and median rent of €445/month. Perpignan isn’t far behind at 9.74%, with prices around €51,269 and rents at €416/month. Saint-Étienne rounds out the top three at 9.61%, and it has the lowest median asking price in the table at €48,339.

What stands out is the pattern: the strongest yields are mostly in mid-sized, lower-entry-price markets where rents still hold up. Roubaix also makes the top tier at 9.49% on a €60,057 median asking price, while Villejuif is the exception that proves the rule—its much higher €109,862 price tag still produces a solid 9.02% because rents reach €826/month.

For first-time buy-to-let investors, the takeaway is simple: small apartments can deliver excellent gross returns, but the best numbers are concentrated in cities where you can buy cheaply without sacrificing rent levels.
