The biggest surprise in Spain’s apartment market isn’t just that Madrid dominates — it’s how far ahead Barrio de Salamanca is. At a median sale price of **€1,550,292**, it sits well above the rest of the country’s priciest districts and even clears the €1.5 million mark on its own.

Madrid packs the top of the ranking, with **Chamberí at €1,149,901** and **Chamartín at €1,101,073**, while **Retiro** still comes in close to **€900,878**. What’s notable is the scale of supply behind those prices: Barrio de Salamanca alone shows **1,567 listings**, signaling that this isn’t just a tiny trophy niche.

Outside Madrid, the luxury map stretches to Mallorca, Barcelona, Marbella, Sitges and Málaga, but the pattern stays the same: high prices, modest yields. In Madrid’s prime districts, asking rents range from about **€2,001 to €2,490 per month**, yet gross yields stay compressed between **1.93% and 2.67%** — classic luxury-market math.

That combination makes Spain’s top apartment markets look less like income plays and more like prestige holdings, where capital values do most of the heavy lifting.
