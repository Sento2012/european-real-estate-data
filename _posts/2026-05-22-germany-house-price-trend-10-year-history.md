The most surprising part of Germany’s housing story is how quickly the market flipped: after years of steady gains, prices didn’t just cool in 2023 — they fell fast, then started inching back up in 2024 and 2025. The Eurostat house price index shows a classic three-act cycle: a long run-up, a sharp correction, and a tentative recovery.

The climb was dramatic. Germany’s index rose from 108.80 in Q3 2016 to 167.40 at its Q2 2022 peak. From there, the market reversed sharply, with the steepest quarterly drop hitting -4.96% in Q4 2022. By the end of the downturn, the index had slipped to 146.20 in Q4 2023, well below the peak.

What’s notable now is stabilization rather than a full rebound. The index recovered to 153.70 by Q4 2025, still below the 2022 high but clearly above the trough. In other words, Germany’s housing market appears to have moved out of correction mode and into a more measured recovery phase.
