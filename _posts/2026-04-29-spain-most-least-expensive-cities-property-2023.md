The biggest surprise in Spain’s 2023 property market is just how far apart city prices have drifted. In the same dataset, buyers could find homes with a median price of just €229,000 in Sevilla or Zaragoza, while Palma soared to €750,000 — more than three times higher.

The gap gets even clearer when you look at price per square metre. Valencia was the cheapest at €1,923 per m², while San Sebastián topped the list at €6,666 per m². That’s a huge spread for a single country, and it shows why comparing only headline prices can be misleading.

What stands out is the market split: premium coastal and gateway cities like San Sebastián, Palma and Madrid sit at the top end, while regional capitals such as Sevilla, Zaragoza and Valencia offer much more accessible entry points. For buyers, that means location is doing a lot more of the pricing work than size alone.

For anyone weighing up a move or an investment, Spain in 2023 was really two markets in one: high-cost urban luxury on one side, and value-driven cities on the other.
