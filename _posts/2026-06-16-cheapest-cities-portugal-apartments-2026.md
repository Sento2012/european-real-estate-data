The biggest surprise in Portugal’s apartment market? Bragança isn’t just the cheapest city on the list — it’s *far* cheaper than the rest. In the 6 Jun 2026 snapshot, the median asking price there was just **€153,808**, while the next cheapest city, **Castelo Branco**, came in at **€246,581**. That’s a huge gap for buyers hunting for an affordable entry point.

The low-cost options don’t stop there. The top 10 cheapest cities all stayed below **€300,000**, with **Santo Tirso** closing out the list at **€290,526**. For buyers, that means Portugal still has a real sub-€300k apartment market outside the big-name hubs.

What’s especially interesting is the difference in market depth. **Moita** had the largest listing pool in the group, with **338 listings**, while **Santa Maria da Feira** and **Barcelos** were close behind at **317** and **316**. So this isn’t just about finding a low price — it’s also about choosing between thin markets and places with more stock to compare.

For first-time buyers and relocators, the takeaway is clear: the cheapest cities are mostly smaller or less concentrated markets, but some still offer plenty of choice alongside relatively modest prices.
