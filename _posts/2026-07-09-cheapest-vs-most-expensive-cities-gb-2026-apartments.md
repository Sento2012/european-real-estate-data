The biggest surprise in Great Britain’s apartment market isn’t just that London is expensive — it’s how dramatically the country splits into two very different price worlds. In the 27 Jun 2026 snapshot, the cheapest cities are all in the North, while the priciest options are concentrated in southern hubs and the capital.

At the bottom end, Middlesbrough leads affordability with a median asking price of €79,174, while Sunderland and Bradford both sit at €85,911. Compare that with Cambridge at €372,289, Bath at €395,872, and London at a striking €530,638. That means London apartments cost more than six times Middlesbrough’s, making the entry barrier wildly different depending on where you look.

The yield picture adds another layer. The cheapest markets are also delivering stronger gross returns: Middlesbrough posts 11.40%, Sunderland 10.80%, and Bradford 10.50%. In the expensive group, yields are notably thinner, with Cambridge at 5.53% and Bath at 5.34%. In other words, lower prices and higher yields are still going hand in hand in several regional cities.

For buyers and investors, the takeaway is simple: location doesn’t just change the lifestyle story, it completely changes the math.
