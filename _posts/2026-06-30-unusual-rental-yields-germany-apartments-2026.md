The most surprising thing in Germany’s June 2026 apartment screen isn’t a hidden bargain — it’s how many major cities are trapped in ultra-low yield territory. The entire outlier list sits below 3%, which means asking prices are running well ahead of advertised rents in these markets.

At the top of the group, Augsburg still only yields 2.97%, while Karlsruhe and Mülheim an der Ruhr follow closely at 2.92% and 2.91%. On the other end, the yield floor gets very thin: München comes in at 2.22%, Hamburg at 2.05%, Potsdam at 1.93%, and Freiburg im Breisgau bottoms out at just 1.68%.

That makes the pattern pretty clear: this is less about undervalued apartments and more about premium pricing in places investors already love for liquidity, reputation, and location quality. In other words, the market is rewarding defensiveness — but at the cost of income.
