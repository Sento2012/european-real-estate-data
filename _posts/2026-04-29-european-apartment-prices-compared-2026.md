Portugal topping the list is the big surprise here: in the 25 Apr 2026 snapshot, it had the highest median apartment sale price of the six-country comparison at **€334,472**. That puts it well above the rest of the pack and makes it the priciest entry point for apartment buyers in this sample.

The middle of the ranking is tighter, but still meaningful. **Germany** came in at **€249,938**, **Italy** at **€232,910**, **Spain** at **€224,608**, and **France** at **€203,612**. Those differences may look modest at first glance, but for cross-border buyers they can shift budget expectations and financing decisions fast.

At the other end, **Great Britain** posted the lowest median apartment sale price at **€183,617**. The gap between Portugal and Great Britain is more than **€150,000**, underscoring just how differently European apartment markets are priced—even before you factor in rents, yields, or affordability.
