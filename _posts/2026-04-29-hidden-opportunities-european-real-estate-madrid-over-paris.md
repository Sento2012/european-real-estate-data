The biggest surprise is just how much more room Madrid gives investors to maneuver. Despite Paris’s prestige, Madrid’s visible market is vastly larger and easier to shop: **34,012 listings** versus **2,211 in Paris**. That makes Madrid’s market about **15.4 times bigger**, which usually means more choice, more pricing variety, and more chances to spot overlooked deals.

Price is another major advantage. Madrid comes in at **€5,705 per square metre**, while Paris sits at **€9,360 per square metre**. That gap is hard to ignore—especially for buyers focused on value, yield, or long-term upside rather than just brand-name appeal. In practical terms, Madrid offers a lower entry point without sacrificing market depth.

The mix of homes also looks more investor-friendly in Madrid. A broader inventory means more options by neighborhood, building type, and renovation potential, which can be especially useful for portfolio builders looking for flexibility rather than a single trophy asset. Paris may still carry more cachet, but Madrid appears to offer the stronger setup for strategic buyers.
