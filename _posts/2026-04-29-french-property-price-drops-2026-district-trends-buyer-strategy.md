The most surprising part of France’s property market right now isn’t that prices are falling — it’s how widespread the cuts are. Realty Pulse found **81 price reductions across 19 French districts in just 30 days**, with an **average discount of -7.8%**. That’s a strong signal that buyers are gaining leverage in more places than just the usual bargain hotspots.

This isn’t just a one-off correction in a single segment, either. The discounts show up in both higher-end areas like **Paris 7th arrondissement** and **Lyon 2nd arrondissement**, as well as more accessible markets such as **Amiens, Clermont-Ferrand, and Grenoble**. On a **€200,000** listing, that average cut works out to about **€15,600** off the asking price before any extra negotiation.

The pattern that matters most is repetition. **Lyon 2nd arrondissement recorded 7 reductions**, while **Amiens, Montreuil, and Nantes** each saw **6**. When sellers keep trimming prices in the same district, it usually means the market is testing a lower price ceiling — and buyers who are ready to move can benefit from that shift.
