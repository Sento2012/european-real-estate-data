The most surprising part of Germany’s apartment market right now is how quickly returns fade as homes get bigger. On the 9 May 2026 snapshot, 1-room apartments deliver the strongest average gross yield at 4.30%, while 5+ room units slip to 3.34% — a clear reminder that “more space” doesn’t mean “better income” for investors.

At the same time, prices climb fast with every extra room. Median asking prices rise from €138,853 for 1-room apartments to €188,902 for 2-room units, then jump to €279,234 for 3 rooms and €577,086 for 5+ rooms. That price ladder is steep, especially once buyers move beyond compact stock.

The rent side helps explain the pattern. Median monthly rent increases from €463 for 1-room homes to €649 for 2-room apartments and €1,522 for 5+ room properties, but not enough to keep pace with the higher purchase prices. In other words, larger apartments may suit lifestyle buyers, but smaller units still look strongest on yield.
