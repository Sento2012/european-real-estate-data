The biggest surprise in Portugal’s most affordable 3-room apartment markets isn’t the prices — it’s how much the affordability burden swings even when prices barely move. In the 6 Jun 2026 snapshot, median asking prices in the cheapest dozen markets cluster tightly between about €246,581 and €275,878, yet the income required ranges from just 2.6 years to 6.8 years.

Alenquer is the clear standout. At €246,581, it has the lowest median asking price in the group and also the best affordability reading at 2.6 years of household income. By contrast, Moita sits at €251,464 but requires 6.3 years, while Palmela reaches €270,995 and 6.8 years — a huge gap for markets that look similar on paper.

That’s the key takeaway for family buyers: headline price alone doesn’t tell you how accessible a market really is. A few thousand euros in price difference can mean years of extra income needed, which makes local wages just as important as the listing price when comparing places to buy.
