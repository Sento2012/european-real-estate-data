The biggest surprise in GB’s 2026 apartment market isn’t just how expensive London is — it’s how much more power the cheapest cities offer for yield-focused buyers. In the 6 Jun 2026 snapshot, Middlesbrough comes in at just €79,174, while London jumps all the way to €534,008. That’s a massive price gap, but rents don’t widen nearly as dramatically, which tilts the economics toward lower-cost markets.

At the affordable end, Middlesbrough is followed by Sunderland and Bradford, both at €85,911. On the pricier side, Cambridge sits at €372,289, Bath at €392,503, and London tops the list at €534,008. Monthly rents are also higher in expensive cities, but not enough to offset the capital cost difference: Middlesbrough shows €752/month in rent versus €2,998/month in London.

That mismatch is what drives the yield story. Middlesbrough’s gross yield lands at 11.40%, while Cambridge is at 5.75% and Bath at 5.38%. In other words, the cheapest cities aren’t just cheaper to buy in — they’re also generating much stronger income relative to price.
