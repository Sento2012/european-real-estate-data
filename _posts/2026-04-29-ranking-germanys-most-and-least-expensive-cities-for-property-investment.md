The biggest surprise in Germany’s property market? It’s not just expensive versus cheap — it’s how wildly different the 133 local markets really are. JobStatsen’s latest ranking shows that premium cities like Munich and Frankfurt am Main sit in a completely different price universe from places like Zwickau, Gera, and Chemnitz, where investors can still get much more space for far less money.

The national average comes in at **€4,494 per m²**, but that number hides a huge spread. At the top end, median prices climb above **€10,000 per m²**, while the most affordable cities are closer to **€1,150 to €1,867 per m²**. That means the gap between Germany’s priciest and cheapest markets isn’t just noticeable — it can completely change the kind of property you can buy.

For investors, the takeaway is pretty clear: expensive markets tend to offer liquidity and strong demand, while lower-cost cities may offer better entry points and more upside if local fundamentals improve. And one especially useful insight from the ranking is that total price and price per square metre don’t always move together, so bigger cities don’t automatically mean the best value.
