The most surprising takeaway from Portugal’s 2026 snapshot is that apartments beat houses on yield, even though houses brought in more rent. Across 61 cities, apartments delivered a median gross yield of 3.72% versus 3.39% for houses — a small edge, but a meaningful one for investors watching returns closely.

The reason is simple: apartments are much cheaper to buy. The median asking price for an apartment was €339,354, compared with €520,018 for a house. Yet the median rent gap was narrower than the price gap, with apartments at €1,141/month and houses at €1,298/month.

That makes apartments look like the more efficient income play, while houses remain the higher-ticket option for buyers prioritizing space or long-term capital exposure. In other words, houses win on absolute rent, but apartments win on rent-to-price balance.
