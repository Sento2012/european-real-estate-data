What stands out most in Malaga’s housing market is how one-sided its price extremes are: there are **zero cheap outliers**, but **15 expensive outliers**. That’s unusual. It suggests the lower end of the market is tightly anchored, while the luxury segment is pulling away from the pack.

The data backs that up. Malaga’s **median asking price is €3,571 per m²**, which already places it firmly in premium territory. But instead of seeing a wide spread of bargain listings at the bottom, JobStatsen’s analysis shows almost no statistical distortion there at all. In other words, sellers don’t appear to be undercutting the market much—if at all.

That kind of pattern usually points to a market with solid price discipline and strong demand support. Cheap listings may be getting absorbed quickly, or there may simply be very little distressed stock to begin with. Either way, the result is a market where the floor is stable, but the ceiling is clearly stretching upward.

For buyers and investors, that’s an important signal: Malaga’s biggest opportunities may be concentrated in the high end, where pricing is more dispersed and outliers are more visible.
