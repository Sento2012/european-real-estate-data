Malaga’s biggest surprise isn’t that it has a luxury market — it’s how much value still exists just a few districts away. JobStatsen’s latest analysis shows an **80% price spread** across **21 districts**, with **more than 20,182 active listings** on the market. That kind of gap means Malaga isn’t one market; it’s several, each offering a very different entry point for buyers and investors.

At the top end, **Costa del Sol Occidental-Área de Marbella** leads with a median price of **€5,824/m²**. At the other extreme, **Área de Antequera** comes in at just **€1,163/m²**. Put differently, buyers in Marbella are paying roughly five times more per square metre than those shopping inland — a huge difference that opens the door to both prestige-driven and value-focused strategies.

What makes this especially interesting is the middle ground. Districts like **Martiricos - La Roca** sit at **€2,692/m²**, while several others cluster between **€3,000/m² and €4,500/m²**. That’s often where the smartest opportunities hide: not the cheapest homes, and not the headline-grabbing luxury ones, but the areas where pricing still leaves room for upside.
