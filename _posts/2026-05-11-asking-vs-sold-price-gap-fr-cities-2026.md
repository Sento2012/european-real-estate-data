The most surprising result in France’s apartment market snapshot is the sheer size of the gap in Cannes: asking prices are running 75.1% above sold prices. That’s a huge spread, especially compared with other high-profile markets like Antibes at 55.7% and Annecy at 50.4%. It suggests sellers in some cities are still pricing well above where transactions are actually clearing.

What’s interesting is that the widest gaps aren’t limited to the Riviera. They also show up in Paris-region and secondary markets, including Issy-les-Moulineaux at 49.7% and Fort-de-France at 53.2%. In Cannes, for example, the median sale price is €479,003 versus a DVF median of €273,500, based on 1,097 listings — a clear sign of how far apart asking and closing values can be.

At the other end of the spectrum, Saint-Quentin is the outlier with a negative gap of -44.9%. That kind of reading usually points less to a “discounted market” and more to a thin or unrepresentative sold sample. Overall, the data looks like a useful price-discovery signal: the bigger the gap, the more room there may be between seller expectations and real transaction levels.
