What’s most surprising about Bordeaux right now is that the market looks cheaper on listings than it really is. While current asking prices sit at a median of **€3,894/m²**, recent sales are closing at **€4,254/m²** — an **8.5% gap** that suggests buyers are facing a firmer, more competitive market than portal data alone implies.

That matters because sold prices are telling a different story from asking prices. The average completed sale is even higher at **€5,751/m²**, which hints that prime homes are still commanding strong premiums. In other words, Bordeaux isn’t really a bargain market just because some listings appear below €4,000/m².

The trend is also moving in the wrong direction for bargain hunters: sold prices have climbed **5.1% over the past six months**. On a typical **60 m²** apartment, that kind of increase adds roughly **€13,000** to the price tag. For buyers, the takeaway is simple: act fast on well-priced homes. For sellers, recent transaction data is the benchmark that matters most.
