Spain’s apartment market has a striking outlier: **El Ejido posts an eye-popping 18.33% gross yield**, far above the rest of the pack. That’s the biggest headline from the 25 Apr 2026 snapshot, where most of the high-return cities cluster between about **7% and 15%**.

What’s driving those numbers? In many cases, it’s the combination of relatively low purchase prices and still-solid rents. El Ejido’s median asking sale price is just **€95,214**, against **€1,454/month** in rent. Other strong performers include **Santa Coloma de Gramenet at 14.93%** and **Badalona at 13.13%**, showing this isn’t just a one-city anomaly.

On the other end of the spectrum, **San Sebastián - Donostia** is the clear premium-market outlier, with a much lower **2.74% yield**. That gap highlights how dramatically Spanish apartment returns can vary depending on the local balance between pricing and rent levels.

For investors, the takeaway is simple: Spain’s yield story is less about the national average and more about spotting the pockets where pricing and rent are most out of sync.
