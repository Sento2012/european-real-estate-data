The biggest surprise in Spain’s family-buyer apartment snapshot is just how affordable the market still is outside the major metros. In the 9 May 2026 data, the cheapest 3-room apartment in the group is in Linares at just €80,566, and even the most expensive markets in this slice top out at only €134,276.

That puts a lot of usable family-sized stock in reach. El Ejido follows at €104,979, while Fuente Álamo de Murcia comes in at €114,745. In fact, 10 of the 12 markets cluster between €104,979 and €134,276, which suggests this isn’t a one-off bargain hunt — it’s a fairly broad band of sub-€135k options.

What makes the list more interesting is that low prices don’t automatically mean weak rental performance. Reus leads on gross yield at 10.94%, followed by Fuente Álamo de Murcia at 9.49% and Tarragona at 9.10%. For buyers weighing both liveability and investment potential, that mix of affordability and rent strength is the real story.
