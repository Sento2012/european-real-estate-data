The most surprising takeaway? In both Marseille and Nice, homes are apparently selling for **well above** the current listing medians in the dataset. That’s a big clue that these markets are moving fast — or that the homes transacting are a higher-end slice of the stock than what’s showing up in listings.

The price gap is especially striking in Nice. The median listing price per square metre there is **€3,409/m²**, compared with **€1,413/m²** in Marseille. But sold prices climb even higher: **€4,951/m²** in Nice versus **€3,223/m²** in Marseille. In other words, Marseille is still the more affordable entry point, while Nice keeps its premium status.

The dataset also shows a negative ask-vs-sold gap in both cities: **-25.5%** for Marseille and **-31.1%** for Nice. That’s not the usual “list high, negotiate down” story. Instead, buyers should treat portal prices as a starting point, not a ceiling — especially in Nice, where the market appears to be clearing at a much stronger level.

For buyers, the strategy is simple: don’t anchor to listings alone. In these two markets, the real market signal is coming from sold data, and it’s telling a very different story.
