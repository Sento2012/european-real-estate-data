The biggest surprise in Germany’s 2026 housing snapshot? **Houses delivered a higher median gross yield than apartments, even though they cost far more upfront.** Across 57 cities, houses posted a median yield of **3.94%**, while apartments came in at **3.22%**. That flips the usual assumption that the cheaper, denser product automatically offers the better rental return.

The price gap was just as stark. Apartments sat at a median asking price of **€249,938** with median asking rent of **€668/month**, while houses were priced at **€599,058** and rented for **€1,948/month**. In other words, apartments remain the lower-entry option, but houses are pulling in much stronger monthly income.

For buyers and landlords, the takeaway is simple: subtype matters. In this cross-city sample, the apartment market looks more accessible, but the house segment is where the income advantage shows up. That makes Germany’s market a good reminder that yield and affordability don’t always move together.
