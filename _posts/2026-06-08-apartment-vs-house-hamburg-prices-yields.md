Hamburg’s biggest surprise in the 6 Jun 2026 snapshot is that **houses beat apartments on gross yield**, even though they cost far more to buy. That’s not the usual big-city pattern, where apartments often win on rental efficiency thanks to a lower entry price.

The numbers make the split clear: **houses delivered a 3.98% gross yield**, compared with just **2.03% for apartments**. But that stronger income return came with a much higher price tag — the median asking price for houses was **€799,865**, versus **€475,157** for apartments. On the rental side, houses also posted a much higher median asking rent at **€2,651/month**, while apartments were at **€805/month**.

So for buyers in Hamburg, the choice looks pretty straightforward: apartments remain the cheaper way into the market, but houses offered the better rent-to-price balance in this snapshot. That makes the house segment especially interesting for investors who can handle the larger upfront commitment and want stronger gross income performance.
