Barcelona’s biggest surprise is that houses currently deliver a stronger gross asking yield than apartments, even in a dense city where flats usually win on return. That’s not a small edge either: Barcelona houses are showing an 8.61% yield versus 6.77% for apartments.

The catch, of course, is price. The median asking sale price for a house is €1,066,894, compared with €380,859 for an apartment. But the rent side is equally striking: houses are asking €7,656/month on median, while apartments sit at €2,148/month. In other words, the premium house market is not just more expensive — it’s also generating proportionally stronger rent.

Apartments still dominate Barcelona’s market by sheer volume, with 8,200 apartment sale listings and 1,489 apartment rental listings in the snapshot. So while houses look better on headline yield, flats remain the city’s main investment and ownership universe simply because there’s so much more of them.

For buyers, the takeaway is clear: Barcelona’s apartment market is the accessible one, but the scarce house segment is where the yield story is currently more compelling.
