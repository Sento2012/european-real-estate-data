The most surprising part of the Paris snapshot? Apartments actually beat houses on yield while costing far less to buy. On 13 Jun 2026, the median apartment listed for €546,386 and rented for €2,320/month, producing a 5.10% gross asking yield. Houses, by contrast, sat at a much steeper €1,495,605 median price with €4,517/month rent and only a 3.62% yield.

That gap says a lot about how the market works in the French capital. Apartments dominated the listings too, with 9,408 sale listings and 1,529 rental listings, making them the clearer benchmark for most buyers and investors. Houses were far scarcer, with just 165 sale listings and 6 rental listings in the snapshot.

So if you’re chasing rental efficiency per euro, apartments look like the smarter play in Paris right now. Houses may offer space and prestige, but the rent premium doesn’t come close to matching the price premium. In other words: in Paris, scarcity is expensive, but not especially rewarding on yield.
