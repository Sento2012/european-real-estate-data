The most surprising thing about Germany’s apartment market in 2026 is how affordable some cities still are — and it’s not in the big-name metros. The cheapest entry points are concentrated in regional cities, where a typical flat can still cost close to just one to two years of median personal income.

Gelsenkirchen tops the list at just 1.0 years of income, with a median apartment asking price of €108,946 versus €48,600 in median personal income. Herne is close behind at 1.3 years, while Kassel and Hagen both land at 1.5 years. That’s a pretty striking spread for buyers comparing where they can actually get a foothold.

What stands out is that affordability isn’t only about low prices — it’s about the relationship between prices and local earnings. Kassel, for example, reaches 1.5 years even with a higher income base of €59,100, while Hagen gets there with a lower-priced €158,996 typical apartment. For first-time buyers, that means the best opportunities are still in cities where entry tickets remain relatively modest.
