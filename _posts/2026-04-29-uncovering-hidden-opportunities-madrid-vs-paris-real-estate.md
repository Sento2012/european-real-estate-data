The most surprising takeaway? Madrid looks more attractive than Paris for buyers who want space, not just status. Even though Paris has the stronger luxury brand, the data shows Madrid offers a far deeper market, with **34,012 listings** versus just **2,211 in Paris**—about **15 times more inventory** to choose from.

That bigger supply comes with a very different value profile. Madrid’s **median price is €490,000**, compared with **€54,000 in Paris** in this dataset, while its **median price per square metre is €4,933** versus **€7,800** in Paris. In other words, Madrid is cheaper on a space basis, even if its headline prices are higher than many buyers might expect.

The size difference is just as striking. Madrid homes average **178 m²** and **3.0 rooms**, while Paris listings average only **10 m²** and **1.2 rooms**. For investors and end-users alike, that points to a market with more room to buy, hold, and tailor a property strategy around actual living space.
