The most surprising finding is how concentrated Italy’s property listings are: **all 14,489 active properties in the dataset come from just one source, Casa.it**. In a market as large and diverse as Italy, that kind of single-platform dominance is unusual — and it creates both a clean dataset and a potential blind spot.

On the plus side, having one source means the numbers are easy to compare without worrying about duplicate listings or inconsistent formatting. That helps make the core stats more reliable: the **average asking price is €431,706**, and the **average property size is 137 m²**. For anyone tracking the market, that’s a useful baseline.

But the concentration also suggests the dataset may not capture the full picture. Local agencies, luxury brokers, and off-market inventory could be missing, especially in fragmented markets like Milan, Rome, Florence, and coastal destinations. So while the data points to a strong, structured view of Italy’s market, it also hints at hidden opportunities beyond the visible listings.
