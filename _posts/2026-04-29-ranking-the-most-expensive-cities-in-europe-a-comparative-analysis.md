The biggest surprise in Europe’s luxury housing market is that the priciest city isn’t Paris or Munich — it’s Santo António in Portugal. That’s a good reminder that “expensive” can mean very different things depending on whether you’re looking at total home price, price per square metre, or the size of the properties on offer.

Across 20 cities in France, Germany, and Portugal, the data shows three very different premium markets. Paris dominates by scale with 47,074 listings, Munich stands out as a high-value German hub, and Santo António leads on both average property price and average price per square metre. In other words, the market leader changes depending on the metric you use.

That nuance matters for buyers and investors. A city with fewer listings can still be ultra-premium if homes are larger or more exclusive, while a dense market like Paris may offer more liquidity and a different kind of value. The takeaway: Europe’s most expensive cities are not just about big numbers — they’re about what those numbers represent.
