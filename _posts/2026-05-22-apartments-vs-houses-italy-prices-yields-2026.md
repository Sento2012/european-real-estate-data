The most surprising result in this Italy snapshot is the house market’s headline yield: a staggering **330.33%** gross yield, driven by a median asking price of just **€1,464**. That number is so extreme it’s less a sign of a miracle deal and more a clue that the house dataset is unusually thin and likely not representative of the broader market.

Apartments, by contrast, look like the real national benchmark. The segment includes **151,741 listings across 59 cities**, compared with just **19,755 listings in 41 cities** for houses. That much larger sample makes the apartment figures feel far more grounded for anyone trying to understand Italy’s mainstream residential market.

On the pricing side, apartments come in at a median asking price of **€229,980** and a median rent of **€895/month**, with a gross yield of **4.64%**. The distribution also looks sensible, with apartment prices ranging from **€150,878** at the 25th percentile to **€300,292** at the 75th.

So the takeaway is pretty clear: if you want a practical read on Italy’s housing market, apartments tell the story. Houses in this snapshot are interesting, but mostly as a reminder to check sample size before trusting the headline yield.
