The biggest surprise in this snapshot is just how far Germany’s cheapest family-sized apartment markets sit below the €200,000 mark. Chemnitz leads the pack at only €88,805 for a median 3-room asking price, and Duisburg isn’t far behind at €110,167 — both translate to just 1.0 years of household income on the affordability measure.

That low-price cluster is remarkably tight. After Chemnitz and Duisburg, the next most accessible cities are Gelsenkirchen at €119,933 and Magdeburg at €158,996, before the list gradually climbs toward the high end of the ranking. Even the priciest markets in this group, like Krefeld at €199,278 and Mönchengladbach at €198,058, still stay under the €200,000 threshold.

For family buyers, the 3-room segment is the sweet spot because it offers enough space for a child’s room or a home office without jumping into bigger-city price territory. Duisburg stands out not just for affordability, but also for the strongest gross yield in the group at 6.32%, while Chemnitz pairs its ultra-low entry price with a 5.07% yield.
