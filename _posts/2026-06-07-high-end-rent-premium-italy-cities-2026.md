The most surprising takeaway is that Italy’s widest gaps between typical and high-end apartment rents are **not** concentrated only in the biggest, priciest cities. Instead, places like **Siracusa, Vicenza, and Messina** show some of the sharpest splits between median rents and the upper quartile, suggesting very different rental tiers within the same market.

Siracusa leads the pack: the **75th percentile rent is €1,274/month**, compared with a **median of €801** and a **25th percentile of €399**. That creates a **109.2% spread**, the widest in the group. Vicenza is close behind at **99.1%**, while Messina reaches **93.3%**. Even Florence, a more obvious premium market, posts a hefty **92.5% spread**.

What this points to is a market shaped less by broad uniform pricing and more by a limited number of higher-spec listings pulling away from the rest of the stock. Milano matters for its scale, but the biggest premium-rent decoupling is showing up in a mix of mid-sized and lower-cost cities. For renters, that means the “average” can hide a lot of variation; for investors, it’s a reminder that top-end demand can be highly localized.
