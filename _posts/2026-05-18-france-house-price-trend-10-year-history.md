The most surprising part of France’s housing story is how quickly the market shifted from boom to cooling: after years of steady gains, the national house price index hit a 10-year high of **134.34 in Q3 2022**, then slid to **125.68 by Q2 2024** before only partially recovering to **127.56 in Q4 2025**. That’s a clear reminder that even mature markets can turn fast once momentum breaks.

Looking back, the first phase was a long, almost uninterrupted climb. France’s HPI rose from around **101.84 in Q4 2016** to **119.27 in Q4 2020**, with growth accelerating as the cycle matured. By 2021, the market was running hot: the index reached **127.52 in Q4 2021**, and year-over-year gains stayed above **5.5%** all year, peaking at **7.05%** in Q3.

Then came the correction. Prices eased through 2023 and 2024, but the drop was more of a reset than a collapse. The latest 2025 readings suggest stabilization rather than a fresh rally, with the index hovering in the high-120s.

In short, France’s housing market spent the last decade moving through three distinct acts: steady appreciation, a late-cycle surge, and a controlled pullback. The big question now is whether 2025 marks a true floor or just a pause in the cycle.
