The most surprising finding? In parts of Germany, bigger apartments are actually the better bargain on a per-square-metre basis. That flips the usual housing-market logic on its head and creates a rare opening for buyers who want more space without paying a premium for it.

According to JobStatsen’s data, **9 German cities** currently show this size-pricing anomaly, and in the strongest cases the gap between small and large units exceeds **25%**. Cities like **Dorsten, Hanau, Gütersloh, Cottbus, and Jena** stand out, where smaller flats consistently cost more per m² than larger ones.

Even more interesting, this isn’t limited to lower-priced markets. In higher-cost cities such as **Cologne** and **Freiburg im Breisgau**, larger apartments still trade at a discount per square metre. That suggests the pattern may be driven by local supply and demand quirks rather than just overall affordability.

For homebuyers, that means more room for less money. For investors, it points to a segment the market may be undervaluing—especially if the pricing gap narrows over time.
