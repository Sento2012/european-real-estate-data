The most striking thing in Portugal’s apartment market is how quickly returns thin out as homes get bigger. Smaller units are doing the heavy lifting on yield: 1-room apartments average a 4.97% gross yield, while 5+ room homes drop to 3.55%. That’s a meaningful spread for investors deciding whether to chase space or income.

The pricing ladder is just as clear. Median asking prices rise from €231,933 for 1-room apartments to €278,319 for 2-room units and €329,588 for 3-room homes, before jumping to €388,183 for 4-room stock and €554,198 for 5+ room properties. In other words, each step up in size demands more capital, but the rent doesn’t rise nearly as fast.

That mismatch shows up in monthly asking rents too. A 1-room apartment typically asks €926/month, compared with €1,122 for 3-room homes and €1,805 for 5+ room units. So while larger apartments generate more rent in absolute terms, they deliver less income relative to purchase price.

For buyers and investors, the takeaway is simple: smaller apartments look better for yield, while larger ones are more of a lifestyle or long-term value play.
