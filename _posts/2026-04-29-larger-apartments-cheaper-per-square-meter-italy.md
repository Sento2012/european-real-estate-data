The most surprising thing in this Italy-wide listing analysis is that bigger apartments are actually cheaper per square meter than smaller ones. That flips a common real estate assumption on its head: instead of paying a premium for more space, buyers may find better unit-value in larger homes.

JobStatsen’s data shows the gap clearly. Studio apartments under 40 m² have a median asking price of **€3,847/m²**, while apartments in the **100–150 m²** range come in much lower at **€2,694/m²**. Across **14,982 active listings**, the pattern holds consistently, suggesting this isn’t a one-off quirk but a broad market anomaly.

For buyers, that means small doesn’t necessarily equal efficient in Italy right now. Investors hunting for value may want to rethink the usual “compact is best” logic, especially if they’re comparing homes strictly on a per-square-meter basis rather than total price.
