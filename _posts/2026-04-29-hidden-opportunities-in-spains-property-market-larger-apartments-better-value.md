The biggest surprise in Spain’s property market is that bigger apartments can actually be cheaper per square meter. In Valencia and Palma, the usual “smaller = pricier per m²” rule flips in a pretty dramatic way, creating a clear value gap for buyers who can stretch into larger homes.

Valencia shows the strongest distortion: large apartments have a median price of **€2,000 per m²**, compared with **€2,947 per m²** for smaller units — a **32.1% discount**. Palma also stands out, with larger homes at **€5,042 per m²** versus **€6,176 per m²** for smaller apartments, an **18.4% gap**.

The pattern gets even clearer when you look across size buckets. In Valencia, studios average **€6,321 per m²**, while XL homes drop to just **€1,664 per m²**. That’s not a small pricing quirk — it suggests buyers may find better value by looking beyond compact apartments and into larger inventory.

For anyone shopping in Spain, the takeaway is simple: don’t assume more space means a higher price tag per square meter. In these markets, size may be the bargain.
