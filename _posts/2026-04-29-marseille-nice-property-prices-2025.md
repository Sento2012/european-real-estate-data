The most surprising thing in Marseille and Nice right now is that sold prices are running well above listing medians in both markets. That’s not the usual pattern, and it hints at either very fast-moving demand or a big difference between what’s being advertised and what’s actually transacting.

Marseille is still the cheaper entry point by a wide margin. Its median listing price sits at **€1,413/m²**, compared with **€3,409/m²** in Nice. But when you look at sold data, the gap narrows in a different way: Marseille’s sold price is **€3,223/m²**, while Nice reaches **€4,951/m²**.

That means buyers can’t rely on portal listings alone. The data also shows ask-vs-sold gaps of **-25.5% in Marseille** and **-31.1% in Nice**, suggesting the market is clearing above the listed median, especially on the Côte d’Azur. In practical terms, Nice remains the premium market, while Marseille offers the lower-cost foothold—but both require a strategy built around real transaction levels, not just asking prices.
