The most surprising takeaway from Spain’s apartment market? In both Valencia and Palma, bigger homes are actually cheaper per square meter than smaller ones. That flips the usual real estate script and suggests buyers may get more value by stretching into a larger unit instead of chasing the cheapest-looking listing.

The numbers are hard to ignore. In Valencia, small apartments sized 40–70 m² sit at a median of **€2,948/m²**, while larger 100–150 m² units drop to **€2,021/m²**. Palma shows the same pattern, with **€6,142/m²** for smaller apartments versus **€5,039/m²** for larger ones. In both cities, the size premium works in reverse.

For buyers and investors, that creates a real opportunity: the headline price may be higher, but the value per square meter is better. It’s a reminder that in real estate, the cheapest home isn’t always the best deal — especially when the market is quietly rewarding extra space.
