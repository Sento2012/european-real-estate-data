The biggest surprise in Italy’s 2026 apartment market is just how low the entry point still is in some cities: Taranto tops the affordability chart at a median asking price of just **€80,566**. Right behind it are **Livorno at €86,425** and **Messina at €89,355**, showing that buyers can still find sub-€100,000 apartments in several urban markets.

What stands out even more is the regional split. This cheapest tier is heavily concentrated in the south, with cities like **Reggio Calabria, Catanzaro, Foggia, Catania, and Brindisi** all making the list. In fact, the low-cost band runs from **€80,566 in Taranto** up to **€130,370 in Brindisi**, which is a relatively tight range for budget-focused shoppers.

The affordability data backs up the bargain story. **Livorno** comes in at just **1.0 years of affordability**, while **Taranto and Terni** are both at **1.5 years**. Even the priciest names in this group, **Catania and Brindisi**, sit at **2.4 years**, still far cheaper than many major Italian metros. For buyers prioritizing value over prestige, this ranking is a useful map of where the cheapest doors into Italy’s apartment market still exist.
