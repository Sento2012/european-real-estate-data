The most surprising takeaway is how quickly returns fade as UK apartments get larger. In the 16 May 2026 snapshot, 1-room flats delivered an average gross yield of 8.69%, while 5+ room apartments dropped to just 4.18% — even though prices climbed dramatically.

That price gap is huge. Median sale prices range from €149,925 for 1-room units to €688,988 for 5+ room apartments. Meanwhile, rents rise more gradually, from €976 per month for the smallest apartments to €3,064 for the largest, which helps explain why yield compresses so sharply.

The middle of the market follows the same pattern. Two-room apartments sit at €207,201 with a 7.88% yield, while 3-room flats reach €321,752 and 6.92%. By the time you get to 4-room stock, the median price jumps to €567,698, but the yield has already slipped to 5.58%.

For investors, the message is clear: compact apartments currently offer the strongest income potential, while larger units look more like capital-heavy plays than yield-driven ones.
