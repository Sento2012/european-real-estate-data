The biggest surprise in Europe’s 2026 luxury housing map? The priciest markets aren’t spread across the continent at all — they’re clustered in just three countries: France, Germany, and Portugal. And “most expensive” depends on how you measure it: Paris leads on price per square metre, Munich is close behind, and Lisbon’s Santo António stands out for a different reason altogether — bigger homes at premium prices.

On a €/m² basis, the top end is tightly packed. Paris tops the list at **€11,000/m²**, Munich follows at **€10,138/m²**, and Santo António comes in at **€9,619/m²**. That gap is narrow enough to show how competitive these markets are, especially for buyers comparing space rather than just sticker price.

The ranking also reveals how much geography matters. France and Germany anchor the very top of the chart, but Portugal dominates much of the rest of the upper tier, with districts like **Avenidas Novas (€8,960/m²)** and **Parque das Nações (€8,770/m²)** reinforcing Lisbon’s premium status. In short: Europe’s most expensive housing isn’t just about capital cities — it’s about ultra-local pockets of demand.
