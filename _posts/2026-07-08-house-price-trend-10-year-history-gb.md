The most surprising thing in this 10-year GB house price series is that the market’s latest strength still doesn’t look like a clean breakout — it looks more like a recovery that has rebuilt momentum after a wobble. The national index is now **103.50** in **Apr 2026**, with **3.80% annual growth**, but the path here has been anything but straight.

After a soft patch in 2024, the index climbed from **98.90 in Apr 2024** to **102.60 by Aug 2024**, then kept edging higher through early 2025, reaching **102.10 in Mar 2025**. Growth also swung around a lot: it dipped to **-0.10% in May 2024**, then later accelerated to **5.30% in Mar 2025** before settling back to **3.80%** now.

What stands out most is how far the latest national reading still sits from the top of the series. The dataset’s visible peak is **121.90**, holding from **Jan 2026 through Apr 2026** in the broader window, which suggests different parts of the market are moving at very different speeds. In other words: prices are rising again, but the recovery is still uneven rather than universal.
