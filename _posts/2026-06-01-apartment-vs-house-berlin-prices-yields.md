Berlin’s most surprising twist is that houses, not apartments, screen better on yield. Even with a much higher price tag, houses post a 3.95% gross asking yield versus just 2.39% for apartments in the 23 May 2026 snapshot. So if you’re weighing Berlin purely as an income play, the smaller, scarcer house segment currently looks stronger.

That said, apartments clearly win on accessibility and market depth. The median asking sale price for a flat is €398,863, compared with €698,547 for a house. On the rental side, apartments also sit at a far lower median asking rent: €795/month versus €2,299/month for houses.

The inventory gap is just as stark. Berlin has 10,639 apartment sale listings and 11,440 apartment rental listings, while houses account for only 3,279 sale listings and 167 rental listings. In other words, flats dominate the market, giving buyers and renters far more choice.

For most people, this makes Berlin a classic trade-off: apartments are the easier entry point, but houses offer the better rent-to-price ratio.
