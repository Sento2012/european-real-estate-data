The most surprising thing in this French rental snapshot is just how sharply the top end pulls away from the middle in a few wealthy markets. This isn’t a broad nationwide trend — it’s a very localized premium story centered on Paris and its inner suburbs, where high-end apartments are priced in a completely different lane from the median listing.

Neuilly-sur-Seine stands out first, with a median asking rent of €2,393/month and a 75th-percentile rent of €4,508/month — an 88.4% premium at the top end. Paris isn’t far behind, at €2,305/month median and €4,166/month at the upper quartile, while Levallois-Perret also shows a steep split, with rents rising from €1,515/month to €2,619/month.

What this suggests is a market defined by product mix: luxury stock, not just higher demand, is driving the gap. Even in these pricey areas, yields still vary meaningfully — from 3.02% in Neuilly-sur-Seine to 5.04% in Paris — showing that expensive doesn’t always mean equally attractive for investors.
