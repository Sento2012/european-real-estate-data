The most surprising thing in this snapshot is just how completely Paris dominates France’s apartment market ceiling: nine of the ten most expensive cities or districts are Paris arrondissements, with only Neuilly-sur-Seine breaking into the list. That kind of concentration is rare even for a prime market, and it shows how tightly luxury pricing clusters around the capital’s western side.

At the very top, Paris 8e leads at €1,038,573, just ahead of Paris 7e at €1,000,487 and Neuilly-sur-Seine at €988,769. Even the “lower” end of the top ten is still firmly premium, with Paris 17e coming in at €599,120 and every market in the ranking clearing roughly €600,000 in median asking price.

What makes the picture even more interesting is that high prices don’t always mean equally weak returns. Gross yields in this elite group range from 3.40% to 5.83%, so investors are still seeing meaningful variation depending on the district. In other words, the Paris luxury market isn’t just expensive — it’s nuanced, with some neighborhoods offering noticeably better income potential than others.
