The most surprising part of this snapshot is how concentrated Italy’s cheapest family-sized apartments are in the south: southern and Sicilian cities dominate the list, and several markets still offer 3-room homes for well under €100,000. Reggio Calabria leads at just €68,847, with Taranto and Messina close behind at €74,706.

That price gap is meaningful, but affordability tells an even clearer story. In many of these cities, a family would need only about 1.4 to 1.8 years of household income to buy. Reggio Calabria sits at the low end of that range, while other accessible markets like Alessandria (€77,636) and Catanzaro (€80,566) remain firmly in budget-friendly territory.

There’s still a noticeable step up as you move north or into larger urban centers. Terni comes in at €92,285, Catania at €95,214, and Genova reaches €115,722 — nearly €47,000 more than the cheapest city on the list. For family buyers, the takeaway is simple: in Italy, affordability is highly regional, and the best value is still clearly in the south.
