The biggest surprise in Great Britain’s 30 May 2026 snapshot is how much better apartments perform on income. Even though houses bring in higher monthly rent, apartments deliver a much stronger gross yield: 7.44% versus just 4.37% for houses. That’s a big gap in a market where many investors still focus on headline rent rather than return on capital.

The reason is pretty simple: apartments are far cheaper to buy. The median asking price for flats is €190,354, compared with €362,182 for houses. Yet the rent difference is relatively modest — €1,132 per month for apartments versus €1,380 for houses — so the lower entry price gives apartments a clear yield advantage.

In other words, houses win on size and nominal rent, but apartments win on efficiency. For buyers and landlords weighing up the two subtypes, this snapshot reinforces a familiar split: houses sit in the higher-ticket, lower-yield bracket, while apartments remain the stronger income play.
