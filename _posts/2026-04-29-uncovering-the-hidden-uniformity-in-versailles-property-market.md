The most surprising thing about Versailles’ property market is how little it varies: in the current dataset, there’s **zero district-level price spread**. That’s unusual for a major European city, where neighbourhoods typically come with clear price premiums and discounts. Instead, Versailles looks remarkably uniform.

The snapshot is still sizable, with **2,474 active listings** and a **median asking price of €183,750**. On a per-square-meter basis, the market sits at **€7,272/m² median** and **€7,166/m² average**, suggesting a fairly tight pricing band rather than dramatic outliers. The dataset also shows an average home size of just **27 m²**, which helps explain why the city’s listing mix skews small.

What really stands out is that the dataset covers **only one district-level segment**, so there’s no internal district competition to compare. In practice, that means buyers aren’t seeing the usual “best area vs. cheaper area” dynamic—at least not in this data. Versailles’ market is less about location-based pricing gaps and more about a single, consistent citywide benchmark.
