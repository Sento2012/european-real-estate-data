The biggest surprise here is that apartments actually outperform houses on rental yield in France, even though houses bring in much higher monthly rents. In the 9 May 2026 snapshot, apartments deliver a median gross yield of 4.19%, compared with 3.85% for houses.

That edge comes from the price gap. The median asking price for apartments is €203,612, while houses sit at €420,410 — more than double. Rents are higher for houses too, at €1,354/month versus €694/month for apartments, but not enough to offset the heavier upfront cost.

It’s also a market-depth story. Apartments appear across 107 cities, slightly more than houses at 103, suggesting broader urban coverage and more opportunities for investors looking for liquidity and rental demand.

For buyers, the takeaway is pretty clear: houses may offer bigger-ticket rent, but apartments currently look like the better yield play.
