The biggest surprise in Germany’s cheapest apartment markets is how far Chemnitz sits below the pack. At a median asking price of just €68,664, it’s not only the lowest-priced city in the ranking — it’s also dramatically cheaper than the next options, with Duisburg at €105,284 and Gelsenkirchen at €115,050. For buyers watching their entry budget, that gap is hard to ignore.

What makes this list especially interesting is that low prices don’t always mean weak investment potential. Chemnitz combines its bargain pricing with a 6.05% gross yield, while Duisburg still offers a solid 5.82%. By contrast, some similarly affordable cities are less compelling on income: Halle (Saale), for example, comes in at €179,747 but only delivers a 2.96% gross yield.

The broader takeaway is that Germany’s most affordable apartment markets are all below €180,000, but they split into two camps: cities that are cheap and productive, and cities that are cheap mainly on paper. For first-time buyers, relocators, and smaller investors, that distinction matters just as much as the sticker price.
