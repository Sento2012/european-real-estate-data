Here’s the surprising part: in several German cities, **bigger apartments are actually cheaper per square meter** than smaller ones. That flips the usual logic on its head and suggests there may be hidden value in the larger-unit segment, especially in markets where buyers tend to focus on compact homes.

The pattern shows up in at least **8 cities**, including **Dorsten, Trier, Cottbus, Hanau, and Gütersloh**. In Dorsten, small apartments (40–70 m²) median at **€3,933/m²**, while larger homes (100–150 m²) drop to **€2,730/m²** — a **30.6% discount** for the bigger space. Trier shows a similar gap: **€4,822/m²** for smaller units versus **€3,496/m²** for larger ones, or **27.5% lower**.

This isn’t just a fluke from a few listings either. Trier’s data includes **54 small listings and 98 large listings**, while Cottbus has **40 small** and **68 large**. That makes the pricing gap look like a real market signal, not a one-off anomaly. For buyers willing to think beyond the standard “smaller is more efficient” assumption, these larger apartments may offer some of the best value in the market.
