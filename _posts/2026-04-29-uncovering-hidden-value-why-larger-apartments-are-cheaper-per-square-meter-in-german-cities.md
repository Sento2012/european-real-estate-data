The most surprising finding? In several German cities, bigger apartments are actually cheaper per square meter than smaller ones — a sharp reversal of the usual market logic. JobStatsen’s listing-level analysis found this pattern in nine cities, with the clearest anomalies in Dorsten, Hanau, Gütersloh, Cottbus, and Jena.

The price gap isn’t subtle. In those leading cities, larger apartments in the 100–150 m² range can be 20% to 30% cheaper per m² than compact units. That suggests buyers focused only on total price may be missing some of the best value in the market.

Why does this happen? The data points to a mix of weaker demand for larger stock and stronger competition for smaller, more affordable homes. For buyers willing to think beyond the sticker price, that could open up a real opportunity in markets where size is being underpriced.
