The most surprising thing in Spain’s July 2026 apartment market is how clearly luxury pricing is split between Madrid and Mallorca. The country’s priciest district isn’t even in the capital: Cas Català-Illetes tops the list at a median asking price of **€1,184,081**, ahead of **Chamberí (€1,125,487)** and **Chamartín (€1,071,776)**.

That puts four markets above the **€1 million** mark, with Mallorca adding **Portals Nous-Bendinat (€1,047,362)** to the seven-figure club. Meanwhile, Madrid still dominates in depth, with **681 listings in Chamberí** and **399 in Chamartín**, showing that premium demand there is both high-priced and relatively liquid.

What’s also interesting is the yield gap at the top end: some of these trophy markets sit near **2% gross yields**, while others still clear **4%**. So Spain’s luxury apartment scene isn’t just about expensive addresses — it’s also a market where location can dramatically change the income story.
