The biggest surprise in Portugal’s 2026 property data is how extreme the gap is: Lisbon’s prime neighborhoods are trading at luxury-level prices, while some of the cheapest markets look low only because big rural plots drag down the price per square metre. In other words, the “affordable” end of the market needs a careful read.

At the top, Santo António leads the country at an average of €11,070/m², with an average asking price of €1,275,579. Avenidas Novas isn’t far behind at €9,396/m², and it actually has the highest average ticket price in the dataset at €1,432,768. Parque das Nações also sits firmly in premium territory at €9,749/m².

What’s notable is that the five most expensive areas are all in Lisbon, and each is more than three times Portugal’s national average of €3,551/m². That concentration shows just how dominant the capital remains for high-end demand.

On the flip side, the cheapest locations are often rural and can be distorted by land-heavy listings, so headline prices alone don’t tell the whole story. For buyers, that means comparing plot size and property type matters just as much as the asking price.
