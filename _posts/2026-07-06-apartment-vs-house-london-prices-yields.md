London’s biggest surprise is how much better apartments perform on income: they deliver a 6.88% gross yield, well ahead of houses at 4.70%. That’s despite houses charging only a modestly higher median rent, so the extra income doesn’t come close to offsetting the much bigger price tag.

The numbers make the tradeoff pretty clear. In the 27 Jun 2026 snapshot, the median asking price for an apartment was €530,638, compared with €833,861 for a house. Yet median asking rent was fairly close: €3,042/month for apartments versus €3,267/month for houses.

In other words, London’s flats are currently the stronger “rent-to-price” play, while houses are mostly being priced for space and scarcity. That’s classic big-city behavior: if you want efficiency, apartments win; if you want more room and are willing to pay for it, houses still command the premium.
