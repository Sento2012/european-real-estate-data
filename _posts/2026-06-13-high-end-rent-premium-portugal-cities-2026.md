The biggest surprise in Portugal’s apartment rental market isn’t just that premium homes cost more — it’s how far apart the top end can be from the middle. In the 6 Jun 2026 snapshot, cities like Cascais, Viana do Castelo and Sintra show some of the widest gaps, suggesting these aren’t single markets but layered ones, where high-end listings follow a very different pricing path.

Cascais leads the pack with a median asking rent of €2,196/month versus €3,939/month at the 75th percentile. That’s a 79.4% premium at the upper end and a 107.1% spread overall. Viana do Castelo is even more striking in relative terms: the median is just €907/month, but the top quartile reaches €1,654/month, an 82.4% premium.

Sintra also shows a strong split, with rents rising from a €1,357 median to €2,102 at the 75th percentile. By contrast, markets like Amadora, Setúbal and Oeiras have narrower — though still meaningful — gaps, pointing to less dramatic separation between mainstream and premium stock. For investors and renters alike, the takeaway is clear: in some Portuguese cities, the luxury segment is running on its own track.
