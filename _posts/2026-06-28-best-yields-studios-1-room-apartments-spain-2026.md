The most surprising thing in Spain’s 2026 studio and 1-room apartment market is just how far Badalona pulls ahead of the pack. With a gross yield of 11.29%, it outpaces second-place Mataró at 8.95% and L’Hospitalet de Llobregat at 8.22% by a meaningful margin — a rare example of one city clearly separating itself from the rest.

What’s driving that? In Badalona, the median asking sale price sits at €131,835, while median rent reaches €1,240 per month. That combination creates a standout income profile for investors. Mataró and L’Hospitalet also look solid, with sale prices of €146,483 and €153,808 respectively, but their rents don’t quite keep pace enough to match Badalona’s return.

The broader ranking shows the same small-unit logic at work: lower entry prices can translate into stronger gross yields, especially in dense urban markets. Barcelona still makes the list at 7.73%, despite a much higher median sale price of €307,617, while places like Linares, Terrassa, Gandia, and Tarragona all stay above 7.5%. It’s a reminder that compact homes can deliver impressive returns — though usually with more tenant turnover and letting risk than larger family units.
