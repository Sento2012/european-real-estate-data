The most surprising thing in the latest Feb 2026 UK Land Registry snapshot is how uneven the recovery looks: this isn’t one clean national housing cycle, but a market split into a clear leader and a slower-moving middle. One series is now at an index level of 120.60, while the others are clustered much lower, between 101.50 and 106.60.

That gap shows up in the growth rates too. The strongest reading is up 7.50% year on year, while the rest are posting much smaller gains of 2.50%, 2.30%, 1.20% and 0.80%. In other words, some parts of the market are still running hot, but most are only inching forward.

What stands out is that the divergence seems structural, not just a monthly blip. The leading series is not only growing faster — it’s also sitting far above the rest on the index itself, suggesting a much stronger cumulative rebound over the cycle. For buyers, sellers, and policymakers, that means local context matters more than ever.
