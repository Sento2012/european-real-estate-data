The biggest surprise in Portugal’s 2026 apartment affordability snapshot is that the cheapest cities aren’t all the lowest-priced ones. Bragança and Alenquer are tied as the most affordable places to buy, each requiring just 3.0 years of median income — even though their apartment prices are wildly different.

Bragança gets there the old-fashioned way: it has the lowest median apartment price in the ranking at €153,808, alongside median income of €23,300. Alenquer, on the other hand, is much pricier at €275,878, but its higher median income of €42,300 keeps the price-to-income burden equally light. That contrast is the key takeaway here: affordability depends on both sides of the equation, not just sticker price.

A few other cities still look relatively manageable. Sintra comes in at 3.3 years of income and Amadora at 3.5, while Castelo Branco lands at 4.2. At the other end of this top-10 list, Loures is the least affordable, at 5.2 years of median income, with a median apartment price of €485,839.
