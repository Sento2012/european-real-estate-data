The most surprising thing about Valencia’s 2026 housing market is just how wide the gap is between districts. According to Realty Pulse data, prices range from **€5,268/m² in L'Eixample** to just **€280/m² in El Rincón de Ademuz** — a spread of **95%** that completely changes what different budgets can buy.

That difference isn’t just academic. In L'Eixample, a typical **90 m² apartment** would come in at roughly **€474,000**, while the same-sized home in El Rincón de Ademuz would be around **€25,200**. In other words, the budget for a compact central flat could stretch to a much larger property in a lower-priced area.

Even among Valencia’s premium districts, there’s still a meaningful ladder. **Ciutat Vella averages €5,000/m²**, while **El Pla del Real sits at €4,448/m²**. Both are expensive by local standards, but they offer different mixes of heritage, walkability, and residential appeal.

For buyers, the takeaway is clear: in Valencia, district choice shapes not just price, but the kind of home you can realistically get.
