The most surprising result in Spain’s May 2026 apartment snapshot is just how extreme the asking-vs-sold gaps are in some markets: Bailén-Miraflores tops the chart at 183.6%. That means the median listing price there was more than double the benchmark sold price, a clear sign that buyer and seller expectations are still far apart.

A few coastal and lifestyle destinations also stand out. Sitges shows a 141.3% gap, with a median asking price of €646,972 versus a MIVAU sold-price average of €268,128. Calpe / Calp follows at 126.8% (€444,335 asking vs €195,888 sold), while Benitachell / El Poble Nou de Benitatxell comes in at 123.1% on the same sold benchmark.

What this suggests is a market where pricing power is uneven and negotiation room is huge, especially in places with premium stock or slower transaction alignment. Even beyond the headline leaders, León still posts a 115.2% gap and Almuñécar 113.6%, reinforcing that this isn’t just a coastal story — it’s a broader mismatch between what sellers want and what buyers are actually paying.
