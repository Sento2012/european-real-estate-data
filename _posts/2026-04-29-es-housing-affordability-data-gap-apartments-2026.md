Spain’s biggest affordability surprise isn’t just how expensive premium apartments are — it’s how far live asking prices have moved ahead of the income data buyers are still using for context. In the 25 Apr 2026 snapshot, the top apartment markets are already in the €437,010 to €725,097 range, while the latest official income figures available in the analysis are still from 2023.

At the top of the list, Calvià leads with a median asking price of €725,097, followed by Ibiza at €705,566 and Sitges at €651,854. Marbella isn’t far behind at €622,558, and even Madrid lands at €549,316, showing that this isn’t just a capital-city story — Spain’s most expensive apartment markets are heavily concentrated in coastal luxury hubs and high-demand lifestyle destinations.

That gap matters because asking prices update in real time, while income statistics lag behind. For households and relocating buyers, that means affordability calculations can easily feel outdated before they’re even finished. The takeaway: premium markets are setting the pace, but the official data buyers rely on is still catching up.
