Spain’s biggest housing affordability surprise isn’t just the price tag — it’s the timing gap. In the latest 25 Apr 2026 snapshot, premium apartment listings are already well into luxury territory, but the official income data buyers can use for context still stops at 2023. That means the market is being priced in real time while affordability is being measured with a two-year-old ruler.

The priciest apartment markets are concentrated in coastal and lifestyle-heavy destinations, not just the biggest cities. Calvià leads at €725,097, followed by Ibiza at €705,566 and Sitges at €651,854. Marbella (€622,558) and Benahavís (€610,351) also rank near the top, while Madrid comes in lower than expected at €549,316.

That pattern says a lot about where Spain’s premium demand is strongest: international resorts, second-home hubs, and high-end coastal enclaves. Even Palma de Mallorca (€510,253) and Estepona (€471,191) sit above many mainland markets, reinforcing how sharply asking prices can diverge from the income baseline used to assess affordability.

For buyers, analysts, and relocating households, the key takeaway is simple: the premium apartment market is moving faster than the official data used to judge it.
