Portugal’s biggest housing surprise isn’t a crash — it’s how little the market has ever really paused. Over the past decade, the house price index climbed from 108.31 in Q3 2016 to 280.21 in Q4 2025, turning what might have looked like a cyclical market into a steady, multi-year repricing trend.

The pace stayed strong for most of the run. By Q4 2019, the index had already reached 146.07, then moved to 157.69 in Q4 2020 and 175.96 in Q4 2021. More recently, the latest reading shows an annual jump of 18.89%, after 11.55% in Q4 2024 — a sign that momentum hasn’t just held, it has re-accelerated.

What stands out most is the consistency. The series shows only one quarterly setback, in Q3 2020, before growth resumed. That makes Portugal less of a boom-bust story and more of a sustained climb driven by repeated year-end gains.
