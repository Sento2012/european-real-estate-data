The most surprising thing in Spain’s 2026 apartment market is just how far apart city prices are: a buyer can go from a median asking price of **€80,566 in Linares** to **€725,097 in Calvià**. That’s not a small premium for a better neighborhood — it’s a completely different market.

At the budget end, **El Ejido** sits at **€95,214** and **Lucena** at **€114,745**, both still well below six figures. On the other side, the luxury trio of **Sitges (€646,972)**, **Ibiza (€705,566)** and **Calvià (€725,097)** all clear the €646k mark, showing how quickly coastal and lifestyle-driven markets pull away from the rest of the country.

The takeaway is simple: Spain doesn’t have one apartment price level, it has many. For buyers, that means location choice can radically change what kind of home — and investment strategy — is realistic. In some cities, affordability is still within reach; in others, the entry point is firmly premium.
