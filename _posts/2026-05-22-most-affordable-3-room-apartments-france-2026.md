The biggest surprise in this snapshot is how affordable family-sized apartments still are in parts of France: Saint-Étienne’s median asking price for a 3-room apartment was just **€77,636**, with Belfort close behind at **€83,495**. For buyers who’ve been watching six-figure price tags elsewhere, that’s a strikingly low entry point for a home built around family demand.

Across the 12-city sample, median asking prices ranged from **€77,636 to €139,160**, showing a wide gap between the cheapest secondary markets and pricier locations. Limoges also stayed below the €100,000 mark at **€98,144**, while cities like Mulhouse and Troyes both landed at **€109,862**, reinforcing how clustered the most accessible markets are near the bottom of the ranking.

What makes this even more interesting is the rental side: gross yields were strong across the board, ranging from **6.11% to 9.60%**. Saint-Étienne topped the yield chart at **9.60%**, while Belfort posted **8.71%** and Limoges **8.30%**. That combination of modest purchase prices and solid yields suggests these cities are attracting both owner-occupiers and investors.

In short, the snapshot shows that if you’re shopping for a 3-room apartment in France, the best value is still concentrated in smaller cities where family living doesn’t require a huge upfront budget.
