Spain’s apartment market has a surprising twist in 2026: the best gross yield isn’t in the cheapest homes. Instead, 3-room apartments lead the pack at 5.79%, edging out 2-room units at 5.55% and both 1-room and 4-room stock at 5.51%.

Prices still climb in a pretty orderly way as homes get bigger. The median sale price rises from €222,167 for 1-room apartments to €278,320 for 3-room homes, then reaches €435,790 for 5+ room properties. Rents follow suit, but not fast enough to make the largest homes a yield winner.

That’s the key takeaway for investors: room count affects both price and income, but not in a perfectly linear way. Mid-sized apartments appear to offer the best balance of affordability and return, while 5+ room homes demand the most capital and deliver the weakest gross yield at just 4.52%.
