Spain’s apartment market still has some genuinely low-cost pockets in 2026 — and the most surprising part is how far below €100,000 they remain. The cheapest median asking price in the snapshot was just €31,737 in Vila-roja, showing that sub-€40k entry points still exist in Spain’s most budget-friendly micro-markets.

What stands out is that these bargains aren’t limited to one-off outliers. La Chanca-Pescadería came in at €41,503 across 83 listings, while Mariola followed at €46,385 with 79 listings. That mix of low prices and decent listing depth suggests there’s still real activity in these lower-priced areas, not just a handful of stray listings.

The ranking also shows a clear split between tiny neighbourhood slices and more established markets. Barri Antic, for example, had 128 listings at a median of €56,151, giving buyers more visibility than some of the ultra-cheap pockets. At the other end of the list, even the highest-priced area in the ranking, Pedanías Oeste, stayed at €85,448 — still well below the six-figure mark.

For budget-first buyers and relocators, the takeaway is simple: Spain still offers affordable apartment entry points, but the best value depends on whether you want the absolute lowest price or a market with more depth and transparency.
