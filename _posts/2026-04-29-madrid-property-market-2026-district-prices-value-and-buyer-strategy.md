The most surprising takeaway from Madrid’s 2026 housing market is just how little “one market” applies here. Prices swing from a median of €11,900/m² in Barrio de Salamanca to €1,900/m² in Zona Suroeste — an 84% gap that makes location the whole story.

That spread means buyer strategy has to be hyper-local. In the sample data, Barrio de Salamanca averages €2.03 million per listing with a median home size of 313 m², while Chamberí comes in at a still-hefty €9,457/m² and €1.56 million on average. Across the city, there are 34,589 active listings spread over 29 districts, so the range of options is broad, but the value equation changes block by block.

The big picture: Madrid is still more affordable than Paris or London overall, but the city’s premium core is priced like a different universe from its outer districts. For buyers, that means the smartest move isn’t asking “What does Madrid cost?” It’s asking “Which Madrid?”
