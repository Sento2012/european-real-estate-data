The most striking thing in Spain’s 2026 apartment yield snapshot is just how high the returns are in Barcelona’s orbit. Nou Barris tops the list at 10.00%, barely edging out L’Hospitalet de Llobregat at 9.96%—and every market in the top 10 still clears 8.41%. That’s a strong signal that investors are finding income-rich opportunities in urban districts where rents are holding up against relatively moderate purchase prices.

What’s driving it? In many of these markets, the rent-to-price math is unusually favorable. Nou Barris pairs a median asking sale price of €205,077 with €1,709/month in rent, while L’Hospitalet comes in even lower on price at €163,573 and €1,357/month. Sabadell’s Can Feu-Gràcia also stands out at 9.17%, showing this isn’t just a one-neighborhood story.

The broader pattern is clear: Barcelona-area districts dominate the very top, but Valencia submarkets and a few lower-entry provincial cities are also in the mix. Even higher-priced areas can still deliver solid yields—Esplugues de Llobregat, for example, reaches 8.78% despite a median asking sale price of €300,292.
