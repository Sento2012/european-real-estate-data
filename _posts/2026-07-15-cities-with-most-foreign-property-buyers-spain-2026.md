The most surprising thing in this snapshot is how tightly concentrated foreign apartment buying is: a single coastal corridor in Alicante province dominates the top of the ranking. Torrevieja, Orihuela, Pilar de la Horadada and Guardamar del Segura all post the same foreign-buyer share of 46.91%, showing that international demand isn’t spread evenly across Spain — it’s clustered.

That pattern is especially clear when you look at the broader Costa Blanca group. Alongside those headline markets, places like Rojales, San Miguel de Salinas, Finestrat, Benidorm and Altea also appear in the same high-share band. In other words, the foreign footprint is not just strong in one city; it stretches across a connected seaside market with established relocation and second-home demand.

The data comes from MIVAU’s registry-based sold-transactions series, so this is more than anecdotal. It’s a useful signal for agents, investors and policymakers: international participation is already deeply embedded in these apartment markets. For example, Torrevieja pairs that 46.91% foreign-buyer share with a median sale price of €224,609, while Pilar de la Horadada sits at €300,292, highlighting how varied pricing can be even within the same demand corridor.
