Bilbao’s most surprising real estate story isn’t the number of outliers — it’s how skewed they are. Out of **17 detected price outliers**, just **2 are unusually cheap** while **15 are unusually expensive**, meaning a striking **88.2%** of anomalies sit at the high end of the market.

That pattern suggests Bilbao isn’t overflowing with hidden bargains; instead, it’s a city where the extremes are mostly about premium pricing. The city’s **median price of €3,928 per m²** gives a useful benchmark, and the outlier distribution shows sellers are much more likely to push above that line than below it.

The rare low-end exceptions are concentrated in **Basurtu - Zorrotza**, where two listings stand out dramatically at **€52 per m²** and **€333 per m²**. Those are the kinds of numbers that grab attention, but they also underline how unusual true value plays are in this market.

For buyers and investors, the takeaway is clear: Bilbao’s outlier landscape is less about bargain hunting and more about spotting where pricing has stretched the furthest.
