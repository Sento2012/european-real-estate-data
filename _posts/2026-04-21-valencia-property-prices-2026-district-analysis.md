The most surprising thing about Valencia’s 2026 housing market is just how wide the gap is between districts: prices range from **€5,268/m² in L’Eixample** to only **€280/m² in El Rincón de Ademuz**. That’s not a small difference — it’s a **95% spread**, and it can completely change what kind of home your budget can buy.

In practical terms, that means the same **90 m² apartment** could cost about **€474,000** in L’Eixample, but just **€25,200** in El Rincón de Ademuz. Even within the city’s premium core, there’s a clear hierarchy: **Ciutat Vella averages €5,000/m²**, while **El Pla del Real sits at €4,448/m²**.

For buyers, the takeaway is simple: in Valencia, district choice matters as much as square footage. A mid-range budget may still open the door to very different lifestyles depending on whether you prioritize centrality, prestige, space, or affordability.
