The biggest surprise in this GB rent snapshot is just how far premium apartments pull away from the mainstream in some cities. Glasgow leads the pack with a 62.9% jump from median to upper-quartile asking rent, suggesting a market where top-tier listings are priced in a very different league from the rest.

That pattern isn’t limited to Scotland. Exeter shows a 56.2% upper-end premium, with median asking rent at €1,403/month and upper-quartile rent at €2,191/month. Portsmouth isn’t far behind either: its median is €1,380/month, but the upper quartile climbs to €2,132/month, a 54.5% gap. In each case, the spread points to a mix of standard stock and a smaller pool of more desirable or newer apartments pushing the top end higher.

What makes the story more interesting is that some of the widest gaps show up alongside healthy market depth. Glasgow, for example, has 1,290 listings, so this isn’t just a thin-market anomaly. Meanwhile, Newcastle upon Tyne and Stirling also combine wide rent spreads with some of the strongest listed gross yields in the group, making them especially worth watching for investors looking beyond the median.
