The most surprising thing about Portugal’s 2026 property market is how concentrated the top end is: the five most expensive locations are all in Lisbon. At the very top, Santo António averages €11,070 per m², with a median of €9,619 per m² — more than three times the national average of €3,551 per m².

What makes this even more striking is that Avenidas Novas has the highest average asking price overall at €1,432,768, slightly ahead of Parque das Nações at €1,429,019. In other words, some Lisbon neighborhoods are competing not just on luxury pricing per square meter, but on overall ticket size too.

On the other end of the market, the cheapest areas need a careful read. Realty Pulse’s data across 209 locations shows that low headline prices can be misleading in rural markets, where large plots can drag down price-per-square-metre figures and make homes look cheaper than they really are. So while the bargain end of Portugal looks tempting, the land component matters a lot.
