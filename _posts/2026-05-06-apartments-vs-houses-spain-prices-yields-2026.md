Spain’s biggest surprise is how much better apartments stack up for investors: they’re not just cheaper to buy, they also deliver the stronger rental return. In the 2 May 2026 snapshot, the median apartment in Spain is priced at €222,167 and rents for €927 a month, while houses sit far higher at €437,011 and €1,591 a month.

That price gap matters more than the rent gap. Even though houses generate more monthly income, the much larger upfront cost drags their median gross yield down to 4.02%, compared with 5.03% for apartments. In other words, apartments are doing more work for every euro invested.

The market sample is also broad: apartments are covered across 148 cities and houses across 141, with 100,189 apartment listings versus 63,950 house listings. That makes the pattern hard to ignore — apartments are the more accessible entry point and the more efficient income play at the median level.
