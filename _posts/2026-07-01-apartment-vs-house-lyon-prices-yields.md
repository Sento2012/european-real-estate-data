The most surprising takeaway from Lyon’s latest snapshot is that apartments actually beat houses on gross yield, even though they cost far less to buy. In the 20 Jun 2026 data, flats delivered a 3.78% gross yield versus 3.70% for houses — a small gap, but enough to put apartments ahead on efficiency.

That advantage comes with a much lower entry price. The median asking price for an apartment was €309,081, while houses were listed at €748,535. On the rental side, apartments brought in a median €973 per month, compared with €2,305 per month for houses.

So if you’re comparing Lyon property types purely from an investment angle, apartments look like the better value per euro invested. Houses still offer a different lifestyle and more space, but in this snapshot they don’t translate into stronger returns.
