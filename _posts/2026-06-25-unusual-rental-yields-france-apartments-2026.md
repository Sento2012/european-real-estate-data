The biggest surprise in this French apartment snapshot is just how wide the yield gap is: Saint-Quentin is posting a striking 7.57% gross yield, while Biarritz sits at only 1.92%. That’s not a small pricing difference — it’s a sign of two very different market regimes showing up in the same screen.

Saint-Quentin’s numbers help explain why it lands at the top: a median asking sale price of just €65,917 paired with €416/month in rent. On the other side, the low-yield cluster is dominated by pricier, more established markets like Versailles at €499,511 and €1,250/month, and Cannes at €476,073 with €944/month.

What makes this list useful for investors is that it highlights potential mispricing on both ends. High yields can signal cheaper entry points relative to rent, while ultra-low yields often reflect capital values that have outrun rental income. In other words, this isn’t one national trend — it’s a split between value-driven and premium markets.
