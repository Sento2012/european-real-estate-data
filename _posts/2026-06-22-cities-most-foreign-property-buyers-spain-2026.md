The biggest surprise is how concentrated Spain’s foreign-buyer demand is: instead of spreading evenly across the country, it’s packed into a tight Alicante corridor. In the October 2025 registry snapshot, the highest foreign-buyer shares in apartment sales all sit in the same broad market orbit, from big-name coastal hubs to smaller inland towns just a short drive away.

That clustering is pretty striking. The top share in this slice reaches 46.91%, and cities like Torrevieja, Orihuela, Pilar de la Horadada, Guardamar del Segura, Rojales, and San Miguel de Salinas all appear in the same group. Even better-known spots such as Benidorm, Altea, and Villajoyosa / La Vila Joiosa are part of the same foreign-demand belt.

What this really shows is that overseas buyers aren’t just chasing a single resort town — they’re following a broader, established ecosystem. Language services, resale liquidity, and familiar agent networks seem to reinforce the same stretch of coastline and nearby inland areas, creating a market pattern that’s much more local than national.

Because the data comes from completed registry sales, it reflects actual transactions rather than browsing interest or anecdotal reports. That makes the Alicante concentration especially meaningful for investors, agents, and anyone tracking where international demand is really landing.
