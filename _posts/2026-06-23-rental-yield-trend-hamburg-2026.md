Hamburg’s apartment market delivered the most surprising result in the rent data: yields barely moved at all, even as income softened. Over the 12-week period ending 13 Jun 2026, gross yields stayed locked in a tight 2.03%–2.13% range, which is unusually narrow for a market that can still shift week to week on sentiment and pricing.

The capital side was just as calm. Median asking sale prices eased only slightly, from €478,820 to €475,157, suggesting sellers weren’t rushing to reprice. But rents slipped more noticeably, dropping from €844/month to €805/month by the end of the series. That rent decline is what pulled yields lower into June, not a big move in purchase prices.

The pattern is clear: Hamburg is holding steady, but at a low-yield level that looks more defensive than exciting for investors. The market’s floor was tested twice at 2.03% on 6 Jun and 13 Jun, while the high of 2.13% appeared multiple times earlier in the period. In other words, this is a stable market — just not a high-return one.
