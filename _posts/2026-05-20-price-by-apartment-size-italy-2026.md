The most surprising thing in Italy’s 2026 apartment snapshot is how sharply yield falls as homes get bigger. One-room units lead the market with an 8.01% gross yield, while 5+ room apartments drop to 5.13% — a big reminder that more space usually means less rent efficiency.

That yield gap comes with a clear price ladder. Median asking prices climb from €112,792 for 1-room apartments to €350,097 for 5+ room homes. In between, 2-room units sit at €128,906, and 3-room apartments jump to €194,823, showing how quickly capital requirements rise once buyers move beyond compact stock.

Rent also scales, but not fast enough to keep pace with purchase prices. Median monthly rent goes from €603 for 1-room homes to €1,400 for 5+ room properties, while 4-room apartments average just €1,001/month and a 5.47% yield. In other words, smaller apartments are the better income play, while larger ones are mainly a bet on space and higher upfront value.

For investors, the message is simple: Italy’s apartment market is a trade-off between yield and size, and the sweet spot for return still sits at the compact end of the market.
