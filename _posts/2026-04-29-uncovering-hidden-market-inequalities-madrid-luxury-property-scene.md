Madrid’s most surprising real estate story isn’t that prices are high — it’s that the market has **15 expensive outliers and zero cheap ones**. That one-sided spread suggests Madrid’s luxury segment is doing more than just staying active; it’s stretching well beyond the city’s typical pricing level.

At the center of the market, the median asking price is **€4,933 per square metre**, which sounds straightforward enough. But medians can hide a lot, and in this case they mask a clear split: there are no unusually low listings pulling the market down, while a cluster of premium homes is pushing the top end further away from the middle.

That matters for buyers and analysts alike. A market with cheap outliers might hint at distress or hidden bargains. Madrid shows the opposite: a stable floor, plus a high-end tier that’s clearly pulling ahead. In other words, the city’s luxury market isn’t just present — it’s increasingly dominant in the outlier data.
