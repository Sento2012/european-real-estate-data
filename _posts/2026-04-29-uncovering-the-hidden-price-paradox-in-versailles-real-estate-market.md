The most surprising thing about Versailles right now is that it has **2,474 active listings** yet shows **no district-level price variation at all** in the available data. In a market this active, you’d usually expect at least some neighborhood-by-neighborhood differences—but here, the city behaves almost like one flat pricing zone.

The numbers get even more interesting when you look at pricing. The **median listing price is €183,750**, which sits above the **average of €173,714**. That gap suggests the market is skewed by a cluster of lower-priced listings pulling the average down, while the typical home is still priced a bit higher. On a per-square-meter basis, the median comes in at **€7,272/m²**.

What’s behind the uniformity? The data shows **only one district represented**, which likely explains why the spread between the most expensive and cheapest district is **0%**. So while Versailles may look like a complex market on paper, the current dataset tells a simpler story: strong inventory, tightly clustered pricing, and surprisingly little geographic differentiation.
