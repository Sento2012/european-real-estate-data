The most surprising finding? Spain’s priciest city isn’t Madrid or Barcelona — it’s San Sebastián, where median prices hit €6,666 per m², more than triple Valencia’s €1,923 per m². That kind of spread shows just how uneven the country’s urban housing market really is.

The top end of the market is clearly dominated by San Sebastián, Palma, and Madrid, but the story gets more interesting when you look at space and value. Palma, for example, combines an average asking price of €1,344,409 with a huge average property size of 358 m², while Bilbao’s average home is just 107 m². So a higher total price doesn’t always mean worse value — sometimes it just means more square footage.

For buyers and investors, the takeaway is simple: if you’re chasing prestige and liquidity, the premium cities stand out, but if you want more space for your money, Valencia, Sevilla, and Zaragoza look far more accessible. In other words, Spain’s real estate market isn’t one market at all — it’s several, each with its own pricing logic.
