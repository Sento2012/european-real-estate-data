The most surprising thing in Italy’s 2026 apartment market is that the cheapest places to buy aren’t just small provincial towns — some major cities are right there at the top too. Alessandria leads the pack, where a median apartment asking price works out to just 1.4 years of median personal income. That’s an unusually low entry point for first-time buyers, especially in a market where affordability often feels out of reach.

Right behind it, Terni comes in at 1.5 years, while Genova, Torino, and Taranto all land at 1.6 years. The price-and-income mix varies a lot: Genova and Torino share the same median asking price of €136,230, but Taranto is much cheaper at €86,425. Still, the affordability ratio ends up nearly identical because local incomes differ too.

That’s the key takeaway from this snapshot: affordability isn’t driven by one simple formula. In some cities, low prices do the heavy lifting; in others, stronger incomes keep the ratio manageable. For buyers scanning Italy for a realistic first step onto the property ladder, the best opportunities are spread across both big urban hubs and smaller markets.
