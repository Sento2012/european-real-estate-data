Madrid’s most interesting rental-yield move this spring wasn’t a rent spike — it was a price dip. Gross yields climbed from 3.86% on 25 Apr 2026 to a peak of 4.13% on 6 Jun 2026, then eased back to 4.01% by 20 Jun 2026. That suggests investors saw better income returns mainly because asking sale prices softened first, while rents stayed relatively steady.

The weekly pattern was fairly clean: yields held at 3.94% through much of May, then pushed higher to 4.01% on 23 May and 4.05% on 30 May before hitting that 6 June high. Over the same stretch, median sale asking prices moved from €549,316 to €544,433, while median asking rents hovered around €1,766 to €1,805 per month.

In other words, Madrid’s apartment market briefly became a little more attractive on a gross-yield basis, but the improvement wasn’t linear. By mid-June, some of the gain had faded as prices partially rebounded and yields slipped back. For buyers screening deals, that’s a reminder to watch pricing momentum as closely as rent growth.
