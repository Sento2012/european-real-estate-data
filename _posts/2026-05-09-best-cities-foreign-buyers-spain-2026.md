The biggest surprise in this Spain shortlist is how much yield you can still squeeze out of Costa Blanca towns without paying coastal-prime prices. Dolores tops the group at a 5.35% gross apartment yield, beating better-known names like Torrevieja and Orihuela while staying far below the €250,000 mark on entry price.

That pattern shows up across the list. Dolores combines a median asking price of €168,456 with €751/month in rent, while Torrevieja comes in at €214,843 and €820/month for a 4.58% yield. Orihuela lands at €229,491 and €830/month, producing a 4.34% gross yield, and Pilar de la Horadada still clears 4.00% at 4.02%.

What makes this especially relevant for foreign buyers is the demand signal behind it: official MIVAU transaction data for 1 October 2025 shows foreign buyers accounted for 46.91% of sales in the referenced market area. In other words, this isn’t just a lifestyle play — it’s a market with real cross-border depth and plenty of international competition.

For relocators, the takeaway is simple: Costa Blanca is offering a rare mix of affordability, rental support, and proven foreign demand.
